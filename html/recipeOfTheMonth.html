RECIPES OF THE MONTH

The Best Chili con carne


Ingredients

  • soy mince or veggie mince
  • red kidney beans
  • bell peppers
  • carrots
  • celery
  • tomato passata
  • vegetable stock
  • olive oil
  • onions
  • garlic
  • paprika
  • fresh chili pepper
  • salt

Steps

  1. Start by prepping the veg and beans. Finely chop the veggies – carrots, celery, bell peppers, garlic, onion, and fresh chili pepper.
  2. If using tinned kidney beans, then you’ll want the beans rinsed and drained. Alternatively, you can pre-soak some dry beans (for at least 4 hours, or overnight). Then cook these separately, and these will be ready to go.
  3. Heat the olive oil in a big saucepan over medium heat. Add the onion and garlic and fry until soft.
  4. Add the spices, soy mince, and tomato purée/ passata (tinned diced tomatoes also works). Cook for 3-4 minutes while stirring occasionally.
  5. Add the chopped tomatoes and the veggie stock. Stir well. Cook for another 3-4 minutes, and bring to a boil.
  6. When the mix starts bubbling, lower the heat and simmer for another 3-4 minutes.
  7. Add the chopped peppers, celery, and carrots. Stir well and cook for a few minutes. Note: I add the veggies at this stage, as I like them a little more crunchy. If you prefer them much softer, then add them right after the onion and garlic.
  8. Finally, add the cooked red kidney beans. Mix well and simmer for a few further minutes, until the beans are thoroughly heated through.
  9. Your soy chili is then ready to serve immediately, or be portioned into meal-prep/ freezer-friendly containers. If you’re storing some for later, just make sure that it has completely cooled down before moving to the fridge/freezer.
